Every week, the MBA Programme Office at the Institute of Management, Nirma University emails out the Term-IV class schedule as a spreadsheet. It's accurate, but it's built for administrators, not students: a wide grid of section codes, faculty initials, room numbers, and time slots spread across the whole cohort. To answer a simple question — "Where do I need to be on Tuesday, and with whom?" — a student has to find their roll number in the right roster, cross-reference section codes against a separate course sheet, and decode the timetable by hand. Every week. And when a class gets moved or postponed at short notice, that change lands in a separate email that's easy to miss. This project turns that weekly chore into a five-second lookup.
